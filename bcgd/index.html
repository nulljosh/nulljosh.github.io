Garage Door Repair

Broken springs, jammed doors, or sudden grinding noises? We diagnose and fix all garage door issues fast so your door works like new again. Our technicians arrive with a fully stocked truck so most jobs are completed in a single visit.

Most Common

Springs

Torsion and extension spring replacement. Springs fail after roughly 10,000 cycles. Signs include door slamming shut, sagging, opener straining, visible gaps in coils, frayed cables nearby, door feeling unusually heavy, or a loud snapping sound. DIY is strongly discouraged due to serious injury risk.

Do Not DIY

Cables

Frayed or snapped cables? High-tension cable repair and replacement. Look for sagging on one side, snapping or popping noises, jerky movement, frayed cables visible near the bottom bracket, or the door stopping halfway. This is a high-tension system -- professional repair only.

Maintenance

Prevent surprise breakdowns with regular tune-ups. We lubricate moving parts, adjust spring tension, tighten hardware, test safety features, and inspect for wear. Extends the life of springs, cables, and opener components.

Recommended Yearly

Emergency Repair

Door stuck or can't close at night? 24/7 emergency service for stuck doors, broken springs or cables, doors off-track, and non-working keypads. Availability confirmed by phone. We understand a stuck garage door can be a security risk -- call us anytime, day or night.

24/7 Available

Hinges

Repair and replacement for worn or broken hinges. Symptoms include creaking and banging sounds, panel misalignment, and uneven door opening. Most hinge repairs are completed in a single visit with parts we carry on the truck.

Keypads

Is the keypad not responding or flashing? We fix non-responsive buttons, intermittent function, moisture damage, and lost codes. We carry LiftMaster-compatible replacement keypads on the truck and can resolve it same-day.

Clickers & Remotes

If your garage door remote is lost, broken, or no longer functioning, we can help. We carry exclusive LiftMaster brand remotes on-site and can program them during the same visit. Repair for no-response, short range, and sync issues also available.

Panel & Section Repair

Fix dents, cracks, warping, and rust damage to individual panels. We repair what can be repaired and only replace what truly needs it. We never upsell or suggest unnecessary full door replacements.

Bottom Weather Strips

Protect your garage from water, pests, and drafts. Bottom seal replacement to stop water, cold drafts, dust, and debris from entering your garage. Signs you need a new seal: water pooling inside, visible gaps, dust buildup, or sunlight visible under the door. LiftMaster-compatible seals.

Rollers

Squeaky or jerky door movement? Replacing worn-out rollers helps your door glide quietly. We recommend nylon rollers -- no bearings to wear out, much quieter operation. Signs of failure: squeaking, grinding, or visible cracks. Warning: never use WD-40 on garage door rollers -- it ruins bearings.

Tip: Never Use WD-40

Homeowner Tips

Safety Tips for Your Garage Door

Since 1991, photo eyes are mandatory on all new garage door openers in Canada. If you don't have these safety sensors on your door, your family is at risk of a tragic and avoidable accident.

1

Stop Using a Misaligned Door

If your door goes crooked suddenly, stop it immediately. It is extremely dangerous to move doors up and down when they are crooked because you will damage the component parts further and risk having the entire door collapse onto your car or loved ones.

2

Check for a Broken Spring

If your door won't open, stop trying immediately and look to see if your spring is broken. Identifying a damaged garage door spring may save you money by preventing you from damaging additional parts as you try to force open a heavy broken door.

3

Test the Weight Balance

Disconnect your garage door from the opener and manually lift it. A properly functioning door should be light enough to balance and not fall down when you let go. If it feels excessively heavy or won't stay put, the springs need professional adjustment.

4

Motor Runs But Door Doesn't Move?

If your motor sounds like it's running but the door isn't moving, you probably have a stripped gear inside your motor. It is important to fix the root of the problem or it can happen again.
