Police attended a residence on a wellness call — a request to check welfare, not investigate a crime. Officers crossed the threshold without a warrant and without consent. The subject answered the door, spoke coherently, and exercised his legal right to walk away. Officers pursued him into the kitchen, knelt on his back, transported him, medicated him without consent, and held him overnight in solitary confinement. He was discharged the next morning with no aftercare. He walked home alone.
